What to do next:
  1. Click Safely Eject Drive above, or use "Safely Remove Hardware" in the Windows taskbar.
  2. Remove the microSD card and insert it into the BeagleBone Black — the slot is on the underside of the board.
  3. Connect power. The four blue LEDs will do different blink patterns — that's normal, not an error. Wait about 60 seconds for first boot to complete.
  4. Connect via USB (easiest for Windows): Plug the mini-USB cable from the BBB into your computer. Windows installs a tiny network adapter automatically. Open PuTTY, set Host to 192.168.7.2, port 22, and click Open. Log in with the username and password you set during the build.

    Or connect via Ethernet: Plug into your router, then find the BBB's IP address on your router's admin page (usually at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1 in your browser). You can also try ssh student@bbb-robot.local if your router supports mDNS.
